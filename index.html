About Me

I am currently a Senior Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Metropolitan University, Sylhet, Bangladesh, a position I have held since 2026. Prior to this, I served as a Lecturer in the same department from 2024 to 2026. Earlier, from 2019 to 2021, I worked as a Research Assistant at the One Health Center for Research and Action (OHCRA), Chattogram, Bangladesh, where I contributed as a data analyst to a national research project on diabetes.

I received my Masters of Science (M.S.) in Statistics (2021) from Pabna University of Science and Technology (PUST), Bangladesh, graduating with distinction (CGPA: 3.89/4.00, Merit Position: 2nd). My master's research focused on Multi-modal Osteoporosis Diagnosis Using Clinical Risk Factors and Machine Learning Techniques. Prior to that, I earned my Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Statistics (2019) from the same university (CGPA: 3.51/4.00, Merit Position: 3rd), where my undergraduate thesis explored K-Means Clustering and Its Applications in Image Segmentation.

My research has been published in leading international journals, including IEEE Open Journal of the Computer Society, IEEE Access, Electronics, Heliyon, Informatics in Medicine Unlocked, and Scientific Reports, with contributions spanning statistical machine learning, deep learning, medical image analysis, and healthcare analytics. I have also received the National Science and Technology (NST) Fellowship from the Government of Bangladesh and secured funding under the PUST Innovative Research Project. In addition, I serve as a peer reviewer for international journals, including Informatics in Medicine Unlocked (Elsevier) and PLOS ONE.
